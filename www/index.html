Election 2012: Paths To Victory

You hear it over and over during election season: The presidential contest is not one national race, it's 50 statewide races. Our Electoral College system means the outcome rests not on the popular vote, but how many electoral votes a candidate can claim. That puts outsized importance on the handful of states where historical trends and polls show either candidate could win.

In recent elections, the Democratic candidate has started with a higher number of electoral votes expected to land in his column, putting pressure on the Republican candidate to win more of the swing states.
